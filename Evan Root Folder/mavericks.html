Dallas Mavericks

How the Dallas Mavericks navigated the salary cap

The Dallas Mavericks are among the NBA's most aggressive teams from a roster building standpoint. The team's general manager, Nelson, is the son of NBA coaching legend and former player Don Nelson.

Nelson joined the Mavericks in 1998, and was direclt involved in the acquisitions of both Steve Nash and Dirk Nowitzki in a three-team trade. It was the first time in league history two future MVPs were acquired in the same transaction.

The NBA uses what is called a "soft cap,'' allowing teams to exceed the caps as long as they use an exception. There are several main salary cap exceptions.

Non-Taxpayer Mid-Level Exception

Each year, the NBA affords teams that are under the luxury tax line a mid-level exception to allow teams to exceed the cap at a certain rate each year. The Dallas Mavericks used this exception to sign Seth Curry to a four year, $32 million contract in the 2019 offseason.

The Mavericks were over the salary cap, but used this tool to sign the player they wanted on the free agent market.

Bi-Annual Exception

Another exception the NBA offers to over the cap teams is the bi-annual exception. This is a cap exception that can only be used every other season. The contract offer can be no longer than two seasons. Naturally, this is among the least lucrative options to teams looking to remain over the cap and add talent through free agency.

The Mavericks used this exception in the 2019 offseason to sign Serbian center Boban Marjanovic. Marjanovic stands tall at 7''4, making him the second tallest player in the NBA and second tallest in team history.

The Mavericks will be unable to use this exception during the 2020 offseason, but Marjanovic's contract will remain on the payroll through both seasons unconditionally unless he is traded.
