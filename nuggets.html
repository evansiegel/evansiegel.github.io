Denver Nuggets

What the Denver Nuggets can do with their cap situation

The Nuggets are going through a re-tooling phase. The team is coming off a tremendously successful playoff run that ultimately ended one round short of the NBA Finals at the hands of the Los Angeles Lakers.

As so often happens, the Nuggets had a number of up-and-coming young starlets that excelled in elevated roles in the playoffs. This led to their price tags on the free agent market spiking and ultimately becoming too expensive for the already inflexible Nuggets. One way the team could get around this is to use the sign-and-trade exception.

Sign-and-trade provision

The sign-and-trade provision allows teams that are over the cap to re-sign one of their own free agents and immediately trade him to a team of his choice. This is done so a player who wants to join a particular team can do so even if they are not under the cap enough to make the signing.

Essentially,the player agrees with his new team on a contract, and then signs the deal with his former team. That team instantly trades him and receives something, often draft picks, as compensation. This exception is useful for teams over the cap, but eager to still participate in the free agent process.

For example, Jerami Grant enjoyed a stellar season with Nuggets in the last year of his contract. The Nuggets are aware of the fact he will have a number of teams interested in signing him, and doing so themselves would push them well over the luxury tax line. But losing a player as valuable as Grant for nothing is a worst-case scenario for the upstart Nuggets.

The Houston Rockets are rumored to be among the teams looking to pry Grant away from Denver. Should Grant decide he wants to play in Houston, he should pursue a sign-and-trade. The Rockets do not have cap space, and therefore have to use an exception to pursue a player as expensive as Grant.

The NBA allows teams to re-sign their own players and go over the cap to do so, so Grant can use his former team to get the contract he desires, while agreeing to be immediately traded to the Houston Rockets.
